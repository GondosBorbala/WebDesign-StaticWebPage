What Do We Offer?

About Us

Our history goes back to 2015, when our professors recognised that there is a need of a quality media education apart from the popular Communications and Politics faculty. At the beginning the new Faculty of Media Sciences held his classes in the building of the Faculty of Communications and Politics, and in September 2020 the university inaugurated our recent building in the heart of Cluj-Napoca.

With our intensive learning moduls you can achieve your goals in the field of Media Sciences. There are many possible career paths you can choose from if you choose our university major. Those who graduated from tour department have found their professions in different fields of media: journalism, media research, movie industry and so on.

In 2021 the Faculty Of Media Sciences was chosen to be the country's fastest developing university faculty. 

What we offer

During your university years you can:
Learn from Professionals

All our faculty's professors are working simultaneously in the media industry too. Thus they can provide more than only theoretical knowledge. Our professors show the students around their workplaces (radio and television stations, editorial newsrooms, film shootings) where you can experience different types of journalism - and media in general.

Study Abroad

We offer you exchange programs with some european universities. You can see yourself how Media Studies are thought in another country. You can have this opportunity twice during our four-semester course, and you can choose your destination each time. But where can you travel by this program? You can visit Germany, Poland, Croatia, Portugal or Sweden! 

Join a Research Team

We currently have 5 ongoing research projects which are led by our professors. Being a part of these projects help you understand many different aspects of today's press. Moreover you can visit international conferences presenting your research and publish your findings in scientific journals.

Career Paths and Subjects

Online journalism

By graduating from this course, learning of the history of press, getting to know various press genres any many more you can find your calling in written press.

Radio

If you prefer studying elocution, voice clipping and sound editing you may discover that your ideal job is radio host, radio reporter or radio news anchor.

Television

If you are drawn by the visual aspects of the media and also you would like to be employed in the press, then you may become a television newsreader, anchor, reporter, editor or even cameraman.

Professors

John Spielberg is an anchorman at News Daily based in Cluj-Napoca. During his university years he studied Communication and Sociology. After that he studied Television and Radio Journalism in London. Spielberg has been a teacher since 2010 and joined our team in 2016.

Amy Gerwig is a movie director who has been working in this field for 15 years. She earned her  Bachelor's degree in Film and Theatre Arts in 2007. Since then she directed many award-winning short movies. Currently she leads one of our projects which centers around the European documentary genre.

Ken Welles is an investigative journalist. His work focuses mostly on political and economical abuses. From 2008 to 2014 he was working in the United States as a political journalist. He teaches at our faculty since 2015 and is the coordinator of one of our on ongoing projects about the Transylvanian political public.

Kate Chazelle earned her diploma in the University of Law. She began her career as a lawyer, and however in 2013 she changed her path: she became a press researcher. She is teaching Law and Ethics at our faculty and leads two projects of ours.

Benjamin Burton is a freelancer sound and video editor who is working for radios, podcasts, online content creators and so on. Back in 2009 he was a radio host in Budapest and now he is teaching Visual Culture and Radio Journalism.

Isabella Dominik earned her Bachelor's degree in Comparative Literature. After that, during her Master's program she was a student at the History Faculty of our University. Currently she is working as a critic in the online media. She is also is teaching Press History and leading one of our projects.
